## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, I pledge to make participation in discussions about my portfolio and projects a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

### ‚úÖ Encouraged Behavior
- **Respectful Dialogue**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Providing helpful, specific suggestions
- **Professional Courtesy**: Being considerate of different viewpoints and experiences
- **Graceful Acceptance**: Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- **Focus on Content**: Keeping discussions relevant to portfolio content and projects
- **Empathy**: Showing empathy towards other community members

### ‚ùå Unacceptable Behavior
- **Disrespectful Comments**: Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- **Harassment**: Public or private harassment of any kind
- **Inappropriate Content**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Discrimination**: Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- **Spam**: Unsolicited promotional content or irrelevant links
- **Misinformation**: Deliberate spreading of false information

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all portfolio spaces, including:
- GitHub Issues and Discussions on this repository
- Comments on portfolio website content
- Any communication regarding portfolio content
- Social media interactions about my work
- Email correspondence related to portfolio matters

It also applies when an individual is representing the portfolio or its community in public spaces, such as conferences, meetups, or online platforms.

## Responsibilities

As the portfolio maintainer, I am responsible for:
- Clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior
- Ensuring consistent enforcement of this Code of Conduct
- Maintaining a welcoming environment for all contributors and visitors

## Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by contacting me at:

**Email**: [ask@gerivan.me](mailto:ask@gerivan.me?subject=[COC%20Report]%20Code%20of%20Conduct%20Concern)

When reporting, please include:
1. Your contact information
2. A clear description of what happened
3. When and where the incident occurred
4. Any relevant context or screenshots
5. Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved
6. Other people who witnessed the incident

All reports will be kept confidential.

## Enforcement Process

### 1. **Review**
- All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly
- Confidentiality will be maintained throughout the investigation

### 2. **Response**
- If a participant engages in unacceptable behavior, I may take any action deemed appropriate, including:
  - A private warning
  - Temporary restriction from participation
  - Permanent ban from portfolio spaces
  - Reporting to appropriate authorities if necessary

### 3. **Appeals**
- Participants may appeal enforcement decisions by emailing [ask@gerivan.me](mailto:ask@gerivan.me)
- Appeals will be reviewed within 7 business days

## Addressing Grievances

If you feel you have been falsely or unfairly accused of violating this Code of Conduct, you should notify me with a concise description of your grievance. Your grievance will be handled in accordance with our existing governing policies.

## Good Faith

All participants in portfolio spaces are expected to act in good faith when:
- Reporting violations of this Code of Conduct
- Participating in investigations
- Enforcing this Code of Conduct

Deliberate false reporting or abuse of the reporting system is itself a violation of this Code of Conduct.

## Privacy and Confidentiality

- All communication related to Code of Conduct violations will be treated confidentially
- Personal information will only be shared with those involved in investigating and resolving the incident
- Public statements about enforcement actions will protect the privacy of victims
